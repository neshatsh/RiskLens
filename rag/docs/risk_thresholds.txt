INDUSTRY-STANDARD RISK THRESHOLDS AND RED FLAGS
RiskLens Risk Threshold Reference | Version 1.0

================================================================================
MARKET RISK THRESHOLDS
================================================================================

VALUE AT RISK (VaR) LIMITS — BY PORTFOLIO SIZE
  Retail/small institutional (AUM < $100M):
    - 1-day 95% VaR warning: > 1.0% of AUM
    - 1-day 99% VaR breach: > 2.0% of AUM
    - Escalation to CRO: > 3.0% of AUM

  Mid-size institutional (AUM $100M–$1B):
    - 1-day 95% VaR warning: > 1.5% of AUM
    - 1-day 99% VaR breach: > 2.5% of AUM
    - Escalation to Risk Committee: > 4.0% of AUM

  Large institutional (AUM > $1B):
    - 1-day 95% VaR warning: > 1.0% of AUM
    - 1-day 99% VaR breach: > 2.0% of AUM
    - Basel III backtesting: >4 VaR exceptions in 250 days → Yellow Zone
    - Basel III backtesting: >9 VaR exceptions in 250 days → Red Zone (model rejection)

VOLATILITY FLAGS BY ASSET CLASS (ANNUALISED)
  Government bonds / investment grade fixed income:
    - Normal: < 8%
    - Elevated: 8–15%
    - High: > 15%

  Investment grade equities (large-cap):
    - Normal: < 20%
    - Elevated: 20–35%
    - High: 35–55%
    - Extreme: > 55%

  Small-cap / growth equities:
    - Normal: < 35%
    - Elevated: 35–55%
    - High: > 55%

  Commodities (e.g., gold, oil):
    - Normal: < 20%
    - Elevated: 20–35%
    - High: > 35%

  Cryptocurrencies (if held):
    - All levels significantly higher; regulatory capital treatment uncertain

DRAWDOWN TRIGGER LEVELS (PEAK-TO-TROUGH)
  Single position alerts:
    - Watch-list: -15% from 52-week high
    - Warning: -25% from 52-week high
    - Alert: -30% from peak
    - Critical / forced review: -40% from peak
    - Extreme / potential impairment: -50% from peak

  Portfolio-level drawdown:
    - Reporting trigger: -8% portfolio drawdown
    - Risk committee review: -12% portfolio drawdown
    - CRO escalation: -20% portfolio drawdown

BETA / MARKET SENSITIVITY
  - Beta < 0.5: Defensive (gold, utilities, bonds) — acts as portfolio hedge
  - Beta 0.5–1.0: Moderate market sensitivity
  - Beta 1.0–1.5: Market-like behaviour, no amplification
  - Beta > 1.5: High market sensitivity — amplifies both gains and losses
  - Beta > 2.0: Extreme sensitivity — warrants position size limits

CORRELATION AND CONCENTRATION
  Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) — portfolio concentration:
    - HHI < 0.10: Well-diversified
    - HHI 0.10–0.25: Moderate concentration
    - HHI > 0.25: High concentration — requires diversification review
    - HHI > 0.40: Extreme concentration — near-single-name exposure

  Single-name concentration limits (typical Canadian bank policy):
    - Maximum 10% of portfolio in a single name (equity)
    - Maximum 20% in a single sector
    - Maximum 30% in a single asset class (excluding fixed income)

  Correlation risk:
    - Correlation > 0.85 between two large positions = high co-movement risk
    - Average pairwise correlation > 0.70 = portfolio lacks diversification

MOVING AVERAGE SIGNALS
  Golden Cross (50-day MA crosses above 200-day MA):
    - Typically bullish technical signal; not a risk flag, but context for review

  Death Cross (50-day MA crosses below 200-day MA):
    - Risk flag: momentum deterioration; potential trend reversal
    - Particularly significant for large portfolio positions (>5% weight)

CREDIT RISK THRESHOLDS
================================================================================

CREDIT RATING EQUIVALENTS AND RISK WEIGHTS (STANDARDISED APPROACH)
  AAA / AA:        20% risk weight  — investment grade, minimal default risk
  A:               50% risk weight  — investment grade, low default risk
  BBB:            100% risk weight  — investment grade, moderate default risk
  BB:             100% risk weight  — speculative grade / high yield
  B:              150% risk weight  — highly speculative, elevated default risk
  CCC and below: 150% risk weight  — distressed / near-default

PROBABILITY OF DEFAULT (PD) THRESHOLDS
  Basel Internal Rating Categories:
    - PD < 0.10% (BP = basis points): Equivalent to AA/AAA
    - PD 0.10–0.25%: Equivalent to A
    - PD 0.25–0.50%: Equivalent to BBB
    - PD 0.50–2.0%: Equivalent to BB
    - PD 2.0–15%: Equivalent to B
    - PD > 15%: Non-performing / in default

  Internal Watch-list Triggers:
    - PD increase > 100bps in 90 days: Flag for credit review
    - Downgrades from investment grade to speculative grade: Immediate review
    - Any downgrade to CCC: Escalate to Credit Committee

CREDIT SPREAD THRESHOLDS
  Investment Grade (IG) Corporate Spreads:
    - Normal: < 100bps over Treasuries
    - Elevated: 100–200bps
    - Stress: > 200bps
    - Crisis: > 400bps (2009 peak IG spreads reached ~600bps)

  High Yield (HY) Spreads (BAMLH0A0HYM2):
    - Normal: < 300bps
    - Elevated: 300–500bps
    - Stress: > 500bps
    - Crisis: > 800bps (2009 peak HY spreads exceeded 2000bps)

  CDS Spread Triggers (Credit Default Swap):
    - 5-year CDS > 100bps: Credit stress flagged
    - 5-year CDS > 300bps: High credit risk
    - 5-year CDS > 500bps: Near-distressed credit

ALTMAN Z-SCORE BOUNDARIES
  Manufacturing companies: Z < 1.81 = Distress | 1.81–2.99 = Grey | >2.99 = Safe
  Non-manufacturing/services: Z < 1.23 = Distress | 1.23–2.90 = Grey | >2.90 = Safe

================================================================================
OPERATIONAL RISK THRESHOLDS
================================================================================

REGULATORY FINES — MATERIALITY THRESHOLDS
  - Fine > $1M: Flag in operational risk register
  - Fine > $10M: Report to Risk Committee
  - Fine > $100M: Board-level notification; potential reputational score impact
  - Fine > $1B: Crisis-level operational event (e.g., BNP Paribas $8.9B OFAC settlement)

CYBER / TECHNOLOGY RISK THRESHOLDS
  - System downtime > 4 hours: Critical operational event
  - Data breach > 10,000 records: Regulatory notification required (PIPEDA, GDPR)
  - Ransomware attack: Immediate escalation; potential trading halt
  - Third-party vendor breach affecting core banking: Systemic risk trigger

KEY PERSON RISK
  - CEO/CFO departure (unplanned): Medium operational risk flag
  - CEO/CFO departure (negative circumstances): High operational risk flag
  - Multiple C-suite departures within 90 days: Critical key person risk flag

LEGAL / REGULATORY RISK MATERIALITY
  - Class action filed: Report to legal risk team
  - Regulatory investigation opened: High risk flag
  - Regulatory order issued: Critical flag; potential capital impact
  - Criminal indictment (institution or executives): CRITICAL — immediate CRO escalation

================================================================================
MACRO / SYSTEMIC RISK THRESHOLDS
================================================================================

YIELD CURVE SIGNALS
  - 10Y-2Y spread < 0 (inverted): Recession warning signal
    Historical note: Yield curve inverted before every US recession since 1960.
    Average lead time: 12–18 months before recession onset.
  - 10Y-2Y spread < -0.50: Strong recession signal
  - 10Y-2Y spread < -1.00: Extreme inversion (rare; last seen early 2023)

  Duration risk thresholds:
    - Duration of bond portfolio > 10 years: High sensitivity to rate changes
    - A 100bps rate increase on duration-10 bond ≈ -10% price impact
    - DV01 (dollar value of 1bp) per $1M face: ~$1,000 for 10yr treasury

VIX (CBOE VOLATILITY INDEX) REGIMES
  - VIX < 15: Low volatility / risk-on environment
  - VIX 15–25: Normal volatility range
  - VIX 25–35: Elevated uncertainty / risk-off
  - VIX > 35: Fear/crisis (VIX hit 82 in March 2020, 80 in October 2008)
  - VIX spike > 50% in one week: Market stress signal

INFLATION REGIME
  - CPI (YoY) < 2%: Below target; potential deflation risk
  - CPI 2–4%: Target range (central bank comfort zone)
  - CPI > 4%: Above target; rate hike pressure
  - CPI > 6%: High inflation regime; real return compression
  - CPI > 8%: Extreme inflation; aggressive monetary tightening likely

FEDERAL FUNDS RATE / POLICY RATE
  - Rate > 5%: Restrictive monetary policy; pressure on leveraged positions, duration
  - Rate increase > 75bps in single meeting: Rare; aggressive tightening (2022 cycle)
  - Rate inversion vs short-term bonds: Funding cost pressure for banks

CANADIAN-SPECIFIC THRESHOLDS (OSFI)
  - OSFI Domestic Stability Buffer (DSB): 3.5% of RWA (Q4 2023)
    Buffer that OSFI can release during stress to support lending
  - Household debt-to-income ratio > 180%: Systemic household leverage risk (Canada: ~185%)
  - Uninsured mortgage LTV > 80%: Elevated real estate credit risk
  - Office real estate vacancy > 20%: Commercial real estate stress signal

PORTFOLIO-LEVEL COMPOSITE RISK SCORES — INTERPRETATION GUIDE
================================================================================

OVERALL RISK SCORE 0–100 (RiskLens Scoring Framework)

  0–34   LOW RISK
    Interpretation: Portfolio within normal operating parameters. No material
    breaches of standard risk thresholds across all three Basel pillars.
    Action: Routine monitoring; standard weekly risk reporting.

  35–54  MEDIUM RISK
    Interpretation: One or more risk indicators elevated above normal thresholds.
    Portfolio warrants closer monitoring but capital adequacy is not threatened.
    Action: Increase monitoring frequency; document risk factors in CRO report.

  55–74  HIGH RISK
    Interpretation: Multiple significant risk flags across pillars. Capital buffer
    consumption is possible in stress scenarios. Regulatory escalation may be warranted.
    Action: HITL review required. Risk Committee notification. Consider position adjustments.

  75–100 CRITICAL RISK
    Interpretation: Severe risk profile. One or more hard limits breached. Potential
    capital adequacy impact. Regulatory reporting may be required.
    Action: Immediate CRO escalation. Emergency Risk Committee meeting.
    Consider emergency deleveraging or hedging. Regulatory notification if applicable.
